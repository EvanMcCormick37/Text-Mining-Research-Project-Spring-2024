Transgender Rights Movement¶

trans·gen·der

/ˌtranzˈjendər,ˌtran(t)sˈjendər/

adjective- denoting or relating to a person whose gender identity does not correspond with the sex registered for them at birth.

In recent years, the transgender rights movement has grown massively, and seen it's fair share of public and political controversy. Stemming from the LGBT movement of the early 2000s, this movement is pushing the US government to enact policies which would give transgender individuals specific protections and allowances. Many people see this as a civil rights movement, in which the trans community is pushing for similar rights to other groups. Others question the legitimacy of the claimed rights, and indeed some even question the legitimacy of being transgender at all.

Many Battlegrounds of the Transgender Rights Movement¶

Gender-Specific Activities and Areas¶

Should transgender individuals be granted admission into gender-specific areas corresponding to their gender identity, or only to the gender they're assigned to at birth? This is a hotly debated topic across multiple areas, such as:

  1. Use of male/female public restrooms
  2. Admission into all-male/all-female schools and colleges
  3. Participation in male/female sports divisions

Health and Gender-Affirming Care¶

Medical procedures for transgender people are often lumped together under the term 'gender-affirming care', although this term includes a wide variety of medical procedures with varying degrees of impact on the body. The efficacy of gender affirming care is hotly debated, with the administration of hormone blockers to transgender minors being an issue of particular focus. For exampe, Alabama House Bill 1, introduced in 2021, attempted to prevent minors from receiving medication intended to 'alter the appearance of the minor child's gender or delay puberty'. The bill died on the house floor later that year (Alabama HB1).

Discrimination and Violence¶

'Transmysogyny' is defined as iscrimination and violence directed towards transgender individuals. According to a survey conducted by the National Coalition of Anti-Violence Programs (NCAVP), black transgender individuals are particularly at risk for violence. Of 17 homicides of trans and gender-nonconforming people counted by the NCAVP in 2017, 16 had been people of color; 15 had been transgender women; and 13 had been black transgender women(Waters).

A Divided Public¶

A Pew Research Center poll recently found that american public opinion sharply divided on trans individuals, and the degree to which their gender identities should be accepted, affirmed, and/or protected by society. For example, while a majority of americans (64%) supporedt legislation that would "protect trans people from discrimination in jobs, housing, and public spaces", a sizeable minority (10%) opposed/strongly opposed such legislation. In the same poll, 38% of americans surveyed said that the US 'has gone too far in accepting' trans people, while 36% said that the US 'hasn't gone far enough'. This Project's Purpose¶

While public opinion is sharply divided on the subject of Trans rights, it's often murky or unclear exactly where the division is between the two sides. This analysis of News Articles with the key-word transgender attempts to shed light on the events of the trans rights movement in recent months, and the differing opinions thereof.
